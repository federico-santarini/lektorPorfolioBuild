From .obj to Riso

A two-day workshop developed for Onomatopee, which explored the intersection between digital modeling and analog printing, guiding participants through the process of transforming 3D digital forms into physical graphic compositions, introducing accessible techniques for beginners interested in type design, 3D modeling, and experimental publishing.

Participants began by scanning everyday objects to extract three-dimensional data, which served as the foundation for building modular letterforms. Using digital modeling tools, these letterforms were developed into an experimental typeface. The process emphasized variation, iteration, and play, encouraging the creation of unique typographic structures shaped by the capabilities and quirks of 3D software.

The resulting letterforms were then translated into a risograph-printed poster-zine. This final output brought together digital and tactile techniques, merging computational design processes with the distinct aesthetics of risograph printing. The workshop offered a hands-on introduction to workflows that move fluidly between digital 3D environments and physical print production, with a strong focus on collaborative making and material experimentation.
