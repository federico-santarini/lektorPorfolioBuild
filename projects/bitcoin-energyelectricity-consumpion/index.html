Bitcoin Energy/Electricity Consumpion

In 2017, Alex de Vries (Digiconomist) published the "Bitcoin Energy Consumption Index," an article quantifying Bitcoin’s electricity consumption. Soon after, Marc Bevand critiqued the methodology in his blog post "Serious Faults in Digiconomist's Bitcoin Energy Consumption Index," offering a revised model. This project presents a transposition of Digiconomist’s model, using it as a basis for visual and structural interpretation of Bitcoin’s energy footprint.

The work is structured on two layers. The first is a direct formalization of the energy consumption model—translating economic estimations into visual form. The second layer interrogates how slight variations between two technical narratives, both claiming authority on the same topic, can lead to divergent interpretations. This dual structure draws attention to the difficulty an external observer may face in validating or comparing models within a highly technical field.

Rather than proposing a definitive stance, the project reflects on how knowledge is constructed and presented, especially when layered with economic, ethical, and environmental implications. It uses the case of Bitcoin as an example of how conflicting interpretations can stem not just from differing opinions but from divergent methods of framing data, assumptions, and uncertainty.
